Mothman Game Plan

Overall Strategy

Use a commander that cares about counters and radiation to slowly take over the game. The plan is to spread radiation, grow creatures with +1/+1 counters, and profit from long, grindy games.

Early Game (Turns 1‚Äì3)

  • Play mana rocks and cheap ramp first.
  • Develop early creatures that care about counters or radiation.
  • Give radiation to opponents first. Avoid giving yourself radiation unless the upside is clearly worth it.
  • Pick out the scariest opponent (fast combo or big ramp) and let radiation start working on them.

Mid Game (Turns 4‚Äì7)

  • Cast your commander once you can protect it a little.
  • Play creatures and spells that reward +1/+1 counters and radiation.
  • Spread radiation around so everyone feels pressured while your deck is built to capitalize on it.
  • Use combat to chip in damage on the player already behind from radiation and punish overextension.

Late Game (Turn 8+)

  • Look for players low on life from radiation and focus them.
  • Protect your biggest threats and keep some gas in hand in case of a board wipe.
  • Use any big payoff spells or finishers to close out the weakest players first and then clean up the rest.

Turn Script With Commander Out

Start of Turn

  • Check which opponents have the most radiation.
  • Decide who you want to pressure this turn (life total or board presence).

Main Phase

  • Play creatures that care about +1/+1 counters.
  • Cast spells that add radiation or reward you when counters move around.
  • If a spell gives radiation to everyone, make sure your life total and board can handle it before committing.

Combat

  • Attack the player weakest to combat or radiation, not necessarily the one you dislike most.
  • Keep at least one creature back if an opponent can crack back for a lot of damage.
  • When in doubt, hit the player with the most resources or the clearest path to winning.

Ideal Starting Hands

Good opening hands usually have:

  • Three or four lands, preferably with your main colors.
  • One or two sources of ramp (mana rocks or cheap ramp creatures).
  • One cheap creature that cares about +1/+1 counters or radiation.
  • One midgame card that becomes strong once radiation or counters are on the board.

Keepable Hands

  • Two-land hands are fine if you have cheap ramp and your colors.
  • Hands with early interaction and at least one payoff creature can be kept even if they look a bit slow.
  • Hands with only expensive cards and no early play are usually worth shipping.
