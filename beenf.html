Timeline

🗓️
1706

    🎂 Sunday January 17: Benjamin Franklin was born in Boston. His parents were Josiah Franklin and Abiah Folger.

🎓
1714-1715

    📚 Attended South Grammar School (Boston Latin).

🎓
1715-1716

    📚 Attended George Brownell’s English School.

🔨
1717

    ⚙️ Briefly indentured as a cutler.

    🏊‍♂️ Invented swim fins for his hands.

🖨️
1718

    👦 At age 12, started apprenticeship as a printer in his brother James’ shop.

🏠
1720

    🏡 Moved out into a boarding house.

📰
1721

    🗞️ Brother James started publishing The New England Courant.

✍️
1722

    💌 Published his first letter under the pen name “Silence Dogood”.

    🥗 Became a vegetarian.

🏙️
1723

    ⚖️ Took over the printing business when his brother James was jailed.

    🚶‍♂️ Moved from Boston to New York, then to Philadelphia (married Deborah Read in 1730).

🇬🇧
1724

    🏪 Returned to Boston for a printing shop (no loan), then to Philadelphia.

    ✈️ Traveled to London; employed by Samuel Palmer and John Watts.

📝
1725

    📄 Published “A Dissertation on Liberty & Necessity, Pleasure and Pain”.

🚀
1726

    🔙 Returned to Philadelphia with a loan; worked various jobs and returned to printing.

💔
1727

    🤒 Suffered his first pleurisy attack.

    💬 Founded the Junto Club (active until 1765).

🏭
1728

    🖨️ Opened a printing shop with Hugh Meredith.

📰
1729

    🗞️ Purchased the Pennsylvania Gazette from Samuel Keimer.

🖋️
1730

    🖨️ Became the official government printer for Pennsylvania and bought out Meredith’s share.

    💍 Entered a common-law marriage with Deborah Read (William Franklin born out of wedlock).

🔔
1731

    🔗 Joined the Freemasons.

    📝 Published “Apology for Printers”.

    🤝 Entered a partnership with Thomas Whitmarsh in South Carolina.

👶
1732

    👶 First child, Francis Folger Franklin, born.

    📚 Published the first edition of Poor Richard’s Almanack.

🏆
1734

    🏅 Elected Grand Master of the Grand Lodge of Masons of Pennsylvania.

🕯️
1735

    😢 Brother James died in Newport, Rhode Island.

📜
1736

    🏛️ Appointed clerk of the Pennsylvania Assembly.

    💔 His son, Francis Folger, died of smallpox at age 4.

    🚒 Helped organize the Union Fire Company of Philadelphia.

✉️
1737

    📬 Appointed Postmaster of Philadelphia (served until 1753).

🧹
1739

    🧼 Launched a campaign to clean Philadelphia’s docks and slaughterhouses.

🖨️
1740

    🏢 Became the official printer for New Jersey.

🔥
1741

    🔥 Advertised his first model of the Pennsylvania fireplace (Franklin Stove) and declined a patent.

👨‍👩‍👧
1743

    👶 Had a daughter, Sarah (nicknamed “Sally”).

🕯️
1745

    😢 Father Josiah Franklin died at age 87.

    ⚡ Began electrical experiments with an electric tube from Peter Collision.

🛡️
1747

    ⚔️ Helped organize a volunteer militia.

🏭
1748

    🤝 Took David Hall as partner and retired from daily printing operations.

📚
1749

    📝 Published “Proposals Relating to the Education of Youth in Pennsylvania”.

    🏫 Helped organize the Academy of Philadelphia (later University of Pennsylvania).

🤕
1750

    😖 Experienced his first gout attack.

🏥
1751

    🏥 Founded Pennsylvania Hospital with Dr. Thomas Bond.

🌩️
1752

    💔 Mother Abiah Folger died in Boston at 84.

    ⚡️ Conducted kite experiments in a thunderstorm, proving lightning is electrical.

    🚑 Designed a flexible catheter for his brother.

📮
1753

    ✉️ Appointed joint Deputy Postmaster General of the Colonies.

🖼️
1754

    🐍 Printed the famous “Join, or Die” cartoon.

    🏛️ Attended the Albany Congress proposing common colonial defense.

🇬🇧
1757

    ✈️ Elected as a colonial agent to England.

🏅
1762

    🎓 Received an honorary degree from Oxford.

    💡 Invented the glass armonica.

    🗺️ Mapped postal routes in the colonies.

🔄
1764

    📉 Lost his seat in the Pennsylvania Assembly.

    🇬🇧 Returned to London as a colonial agent.

📊
1765

    🏛️ The Stamp Act passed; Charted the Gulf Stream.

💼
1766

    🏭 Sold his printing business to David Hall.

👰
1767

    💍 Daughter Sarah married Richard Bache.

🏛️
1769

    👑 Elected president of the American Philosophical Society (served until his death).

📖
1771

    📚 Began writing his autobiography.

⚖️
1773

    ⚖️ Hutchinson Affair.

💔
1774

    💔 Deborah Read died in Philadelphia.

🏛️
1775

    🗳️ Elected as Pennsylvania delegate to the Second Continental Congress.

    📮 Elected Postmaster General of the Colonies.

    ⚔️ King George III declared rebellion.

🖋️
1776

    ✒️ Part of the committee that drafted the Declaration of Independence.

    🇫🇷 Served as a commissioner at the French Court.

🤝
1778

    🤝 Negotiated the Treaty of Alliance with France.

🤝
1783

    ✍️ Signed the Treaty of Paris ending the war with Great Britain.

📝
1784

    💡 Wrote “An Economical Project for Diminishing the Cost of Light” proposing Daylight Savings Time.

👓
1785

    👓 Described his invention of bifocals.

    🏠 Returned to the United States; elected President of the Pennsylvania Executive Council.

📚
1786

    📚 Invented an instrument for taking books down from a library shelf.

🖊️
1787

    🖊️ Signed the United States Constitution.

📜
1788

    📜 Wrote his will, leaving most of his estate to his daughter Sarah.

✊
1789

    ✊ Elected president of the Pennsylvania Society for Promoting the Abolition of Slavery.

    📝 Submitted the first antislavery petition to Congress.

⚰️
1790

    ☠️ April 17 – Died at age 84; buried in Christ Church, Philadelphia. Cause: pleurisy.
