Why Native Plants?

Native plants are the foundation of a healthy ecosystem. Unlike exotic ornamentals from other continents, native species have co-evolved with local pollinators for thousands of years.

Ecological Powerhouses

Meet the Natives

Host Plant

Butterfly Weed

Asclepias tuberosa

The only food source for Monarch butterfly caterpillars. Its vibrant orange blooms also attract native bees and hummingbirds. Drought tolerant and stunning.

Keystone Species

White Oak

Quercus alba

The single most important plant for North American biodiversity. Oaks support over 500 species of caterpillars, fueling the entire food web for birds and mammals.

Winter Food

Coneflower

Echinacea purpurea

A pollinator magnet in summer. In winter, we leave the seed heads standing to provide essential, oil-rich food for Goldfinches and other winter birds.
